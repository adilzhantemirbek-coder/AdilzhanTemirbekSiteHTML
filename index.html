In Japan, particularly in ultra-busy cities like Tokyo, energy is being harvested from something as simple as walking. Using piezoelectric mats and tiles installed beneath the floor, every step taken by a commuter becomes a source of power. Shibuya Station (2008) A 90 cm² mat captured footstep energy to power a nearby holiday light display and a digital counter showing real-time electricity generation. Tokyo Station (East Japan Railway Co.) Piezoelectric pads under ticket gates turn the pressure from each person’s step into usable electricity, improving the station’s energy profile without disrupting its flow.
